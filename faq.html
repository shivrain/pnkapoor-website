Getting started.

Yes. Roughly 60% of our active brands started conversations with us before incorporating their company. We don't need a registered business, a logo, or a deck. We need a rough idea of the product, a directional budget, and a timeline.

The first call is always free, and we sign an NDA before any technical specifics get discussed.

A 30–60 minute discovery call — usually on Zoom. We'll cover:

  • Category and product type you're imagining
  • Target customer and positioning
  • Reference products (competitors you want to beat or learn from)
  • Budget range and target retail price
  • Launch timeline and channel strategy

You don't need to have polished answers — we'll extract what we need by asking the right questions.

From a first call to a signed NDA: typically 48 hours. From NDA to a formal written brief: another 5–7 business days. From there, our standard 12-week clock starts.

If you have a deadline — a beauty event in 4 months, a marketplace listing window — tell us upfront. We've compressed timelines to 9 weeks for the right project, though we don't recommend it.

Not required, but encouraged. Roughly half our active brands have never visited the facility. Everything we do — from samples to documentation — can be coordinated remotely.

That said, founders who visit consistently tell us it changes the relationship. You meet the chemists working on your formula, see the production line that will scale your brand, and ask questions you didn't know to ask. We host factory tours Monday–Saturday, 10 AM to 7 PM.

About 80% of our brands are not based in Delhi. We've shipped samples and finished product to founders across Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, Chennai, Kolkata, and 27 international destinations.

Location doesn't change how we work — only how often we meet in person.

MOQ & pricing.

500 units per SKU for most categories — including skincare, haircare, bodycare, body mists, and tinted range. This is 10× lower than the typical industry standard of 5,000 units.

Perfumes (EDP/EDT) start at 250 units due to the higher per-unit value. Professional (B2B) lines start at 200 units. We do not go below these.

Yes. After our first call, you'll get an indicative cost sheet within 5 business days. It includes formulation cost per unit, packaging cost estimates (based on the format you choose), plus a directional total for your batch size.

Final pricing locks in after the formula is approved and packaging vendors are confirmed — typically by Week 7 of the process.

It varies significantly by category and complexity, but here's a rough range to set expectations:

  • Basic cleansers, oils, lotions: ₹40–₹120 per unit
  • Advanced skincare (serums, treatments): ₹80–₹250 per unit
  • Premium perfumes (EDP): ₹200–₹600 per unit
  • Tinted range, hybrid products: ₹100–₹300 per unit

These exclude packaging and design. Unit cost drops 30–50% at 5,000+ unit re-orders.

Standard split is:

  • 50% deposit at the start of R&D (Week 2)
  • 50% balance upon QC clearance, before dispatch (Week 12)

For first-time brands, we can split the balance further into two payments. For high-trust repeat orders, we offer 30-day credit terms.

No. Our quotes include formulation, manufacturing, primary packaging, basic labeling, stability testing, and QC. The only costs not included by default are:

  • Custom packaging tooling (only if you want fully bespoke moulds)
  • Secondary packaging (outer boxes) if you want a premium tier
  • Express shipping or expedited timelines

Anything outside the quote gets flagged in writing before we proceed. No surprise invoices.

Formulation & R&D.

Yes, fully. Bespoke formulations developed under contract belong to your brand. We don't reuse your formula for other clients without your written permission, and if you ever leave us, we'll hand over the complete formulation document to your next manufacturer.

This is different from our base formulations (in the Formulation Finder), which are starting points anyone can customize. The distinction is clearly noted in your contract.

We can match the texture, scent, and overall experience of a reference product — and usually improve on the active ingredient profile. We don't reverse-engineer patented formulations or violate IP.

The cleanest way is to bring us a sample of what you like, tell us specifically what you'd change (lighter texture? higher actives? cleaner ingredient list?), and we'll develop a "better" version.

The standard process includes:

  • Round 1: Three initial variants developed and shipped to you (Week 4)
  • Round 2: One refinement round based on your feedback (Week 6)

Additional rounds beyond this are possible but add 2–3 weeks per round and ₹15,000–₹40,000 in R&D fees, depending on complexity. Most brands lock in after Round 2.

Yes — this is our Contract Manufacturing service. You bring the formula (either as a finished spec or as a reference recipe), and we adapt it for scale manufacturing in our facility.

We'll run stability testing on your formula, suggest manufacturability improvements (some lab formulas don't scale cleanly), and produce per your spec. Your formula stays your IP throughout.

We have vetted supplier relationships across:

  • Skincare actives: niacinamide, vitamin C variants, retinol & retinaldehyde, peptides, hyaluronic acid, ceramides, AHAs, BHAs, bakuchiol, alpha-arbutin, tranexamic acid
  • Ayurvedic ingredients: bhringraj, amla, brahmi, neem, tulsi, ashwagandha, manjishtha
  • Natural oils & butters: shea, cocoa, mango butter, argan, jojoba, sweet almond, marula
  • Fragrance houses: imported (Givaudan, IFF, Symrise) and domestic — over 2,000 scent profiles

If you need something specific that's not in our standard inventory, we can source it — adds 1–2 weeks to the timeline.

Packaging & design.

Both options work. Most founders use our pre-vetted vendor network — it's faster, the unit economics are better, and we've already tested compatibility with our formulations.

If you want fully custom packaging (your own moulds, special finishes), bring it to us. We'll need samples for compatibility testing, and the timeline extends by 2–4 weeks for first-time custom orders.

Rough cost ranges (for 500-unit batches):

  • Standard PET bottles: ₹8–₹25 per unit
  • Glass bottles (basic): ₹35–₹80 per unit
  • Glass with custom finish: ₹80–₹180 per unit
  • Airless pumps (premium): ₹60–₹140 per unit
  • Fully custom moulded: ₹150+ per unit + tooling costs of ₹2–₹8L

For first launches, we strongly recommend starting with vetted standard packaging — you can always upgrade after product-market fit.

We can refer you to design partners we trust, but we don't run an in-house design studio. We've found that brand design is best owned by the founder — we focus on what we do well (formulation, manufacturing).

What we do provide: dieline templates for your designer to work with, print-ready file specifications, and pre-production label sample reviews before mass printing.

Yes. For bottle colors, cap colors, and label printing, we Pantone-match to your brand specifications. Note: matching exact Pantone shades on different substrates (frosted glass vs. PET vs. matte tube) has tolerances — we'll send pre-production samples before bulk printing.

Available on request. Most first-time brands skip secondary packaging at launch (saves 15–20% on per-unit cost), then add it once you have channel data — e.g., when you go retail or want a more premium unboxing experience.

For outer cartons / shippers, we provide standard kraft boxes by default. Custom printed shippers add ₹6–₹15 per unit depending on volume.

Quality & certifications.

Our Mayapuri facility holds:

  • FDA — US Food and Drug Administration registered
  • ISO 22716 — Good Manufacturing Practices for Cosmetics
  • GMP — Schedule M compliant
  • HALAL — for Middle East and Southeast Asia export

We can also support products that need Kosher or USDA Organic certification — we'll route to certified raw materials and segregate production runs accordingly.

Every shipment includes:

  • Certificate of Analysis (COA) — batch-specific test results
  • Stability testing report (for new formulations)
  • Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS)
  • Free Sale Certificate (for export)
  • Complete ingredient declaration (INCI list)
  • Batch manufacturing record (on request)

This is the documentation that holds up under marketplace audits (Amazon, Sephora, Boots), retail compliance checks, and customs clearance.

We run accelerated stability tests that simulate 12 months of shelf life in 4 weeks. Products are stored at elevated temperature (40°C) and humidity (75% RH), and tested at weekly intervals for:

  • Physical changes (color, texture, separation)
  • pH drift
  • Microbial contamination
  • Active ingredient degradation

Long-term (real-time) stability testing is also available for products where 24-month shelf life claims are needed.

By default, we do not test on animals — no products manufactured at our facility are subject to animal testing. About 70% of our standard formulations are also fully vegan.

If you want a specifically vegan, cruelty-free, or PETA-certified line, flag this at the brief stage. We'll route raw materials through vegan-certified suppliers and provide the documentation needed for third-party certification.

We hold sample stock from every batch for 24 months. If you receive customer complaints about a specific batch, we can pull our reference sample and investigate.

If a quality issue is traced back to our manufacturing, we replace or refund the affected units at our cost. This has happened twice in 4 years — both resolved within 2 weeks.

Export & logistics.

We currently ship to 27 countries across:

  • North America: US, Canada
  • Europe: UK, Germany, France, Netherlands, Italy, Spain
  • Middle East: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Oman, Bahrain
  • Asia-Pacific: Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Philippines, Australia, New Zealand, Japan
  • Africa: South Africa, Kenya, Nigeria, Egypt, Mauritius

Different regions have different ingredient compliance requirements (EU, US, GCC, ASEAN). We adjust formulations to meet each region's INCI rules.

Yes. For export shipments, we provide commercial invoices, packing lists, certificates of origin, free sale certificates, COA, MSDS, and any country-specific certifications (HALAL, etc.).

We do not handle the import documentation on your end — your destination country's compliance, customs clearance, and product registration are your responsibility, though we provide all the source documents you'll need.

Within India: 3–7 business days from QC clearance to delivery, depending on destination. International by air freight: 7–14 days. International by sea freight (for larger orders): 4–8 weeks depending on destination.

We partner with established logistics providers (DHL, FedEx, regional sea freight forwarders). Shipping costs are passed through at cost.

Yes. We've shipped directly to Amazon FBA warehouses in the US, UK, and India, plus to Nykaa warehouses and to D2C 3PL providers across multiple markets.

For this, we'll need your warehouse account details, labeling requirements (FNSKU, etc.), and any prep specifications. We can also coordinate with your 3PL directly if you provide an introduction.

Standard re-order SLA is 3 weeks from PO to dispatch, once your formulation is locked and your packaging vendors are stable. We hold safety stock of your specific raw materials for ongoing brands, which is what makes the fast turn possible.

For brands with consistent monthly demand, we can set up a rolling production schedule — your re-orders effectively become "always in production."

Partnership terms.

From our side: no. You're free to work with other manufacturers in parallel. Most of our brands eventually consolidate manufacturing with us, but we never demand exclusivity as a condition of working together.

From your side: if we develop a bespoke formula for you under contract, we don't sell that exact formula to other clients. You have effective category exclusivity on your custom IP.

You leave. We transfer the complete formulation file (full INCI breakdown, supplier sources, manufacturing process notes) to your next manufacturer, free of charge.

We've had 2 brands in 4 years leave (both grew to scales we couldn't support fast enough). Both came back within 18 months. We'd rather you leave clean than feel stuck — relationships are stickier when they're optional.

No. We're a fee-for-service manufacturer. We don't take equity, royalties, or revenue share. We don't run a brand incubator. Your cap table stays clean.

The only commercial relationship between us is the manufacturing contract, priced at the unit economics we agree to upfront.

NDAs are bilateral and bind both sides. Your formulations, business model, launch plans, and any technical IP are kept confidential from all other clients — even if they're in your category.

We currently work with multiple brands in skincare, haircare, and tinted ranges simultaneously without conflict. Internally, project teams are siloed; chemists working on competing formulas don't share information.

A standard Manufacturing Services Agreement, typically 8–12 pages. Covers:

  • Scope (categories, SKUs, MOQs)
  • IP ownership (formulations belong to you)
  • Confidentiality (mutual NDAs)
  • Quality standards (referenced to your spec sheets)
  • Payment terms (50/50 standard split)
  • Liability and indemnity
  • Termination clauses (90-day notice either side)

We share a template after the first call. Lawyers can review it on your side without changing the commercial terms.
